Census Data

Included in the Regina distribution are topology data files for several different censuses of triangulations. These census data files can be found in the examples/ directory of the source distribution, or in /usr/share/regina/examples/ once Regina is installed (this may differ with your installation).

These topology data files, as well as additional files that are too large to ship with Regina, are made available here for download.

Each census data file includes a text packet containing a full description of the census as well as its origin. Data from other sources (such as the hyperbolic census shipped with SnapPea) has been converted into Regina data files.

PhD Thesis and Related Articles

The author's PhD thesis was submitted in May 2003 and passed in September 2003, and contains detailed descriptions of many of the topological structures, concepts and algorithms used in Regina.

This thesis contains 306 pages and is formatted for A4 paper. It can be downloaded in either of the following formats.

  • PDF (1.8 Mb)
  • Gzipped Postscript (1.1 Mb)

The following preprints which contain or extend specific portions of the thesis are available from arXiv.org.

  • Face pairing graphs and 3-manifold enumeration, math.GT/0307382: Contains generalisations of Sections 2.6 and 2.7 including both orientable and non-orientable cases.
  • Structures of small closed non-orientable 3-manifold triangulations, math.GT/0311113: Contains extensions of Chapters 2 and 3 including a 7-tetrahedron closed non-orientable census.
