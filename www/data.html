Census Data

Included in the Regina distribution are topology data files for several different censuses of triangulations. These census data files can be found in the examples/ directory of the source distribution, or in /usr/share/regina/examples/ once Regina is installed (this may differ with your installation).

Many of these data files can also be accessed directly through the Open Example menu when running Regina.

For users with stripped-down installations or older versions of Regina, these data files are made available here for download. Some additional files are also offered here that are too large to ship with Regina.

Each census data file includes a text packet containing a full description of the census as well as its origin and literary references where appropriate. Data from external sources (such as the hyperbolic census data shipped with SnapPea) has been converted into Regina data files.

Related Articles and PhD Thesis

The following papers either make significant use of Regina or describe some of the algorithms that it implements.
  • Observations from the 8-tetrahedron non-orientable census, To appear in Experiment. Math., math.GT/0509345, September 2005
  • Face pairing graphs and 3-manifold enumeration, J. Knot Theory Ramifications 13 (2004), no. 8, 1057–1101
  • Structures of small closed non-orientable 3-manifold triangulations, To appear in J. Knot Theory Ramifications, math.GT/0311113, November 2003

The author's PhD thesis contains detailed descriptions of many of the topological structures, concepts and algorithms used in Regina. This thesis contains 306 pages and is formatted for A4 paper. It can be downloaded in either of the following formats.

  • PDF (1.8 Mb)
  • Gzipped Postscript (1.1 Mb)
